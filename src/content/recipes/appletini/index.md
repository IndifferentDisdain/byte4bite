On our first date, my wife ordered an appletini. She was 23; give her a break. It's remained her fancy drink of choice ever since, and once we had kids and stopped getting out for fancy drinks very often, we figured out how to make them at home. They're easy, they're good, and they cost a fraction of what a bar charges.

A few things worth knowing before you start:

**Ratios matter in cocktails.** The classic appletini is 2:1 vodka to Apple Pucker. That balance — the clean spirit forward, the liqueur behind it — is what keeps it from tasting like candy. Scale up or down, but hold the ratio.

**Chill the glass.** Since there's no ice in the finished drink, a cold glass is the only thing standing between you and a room-temperature cocktail ten minutes in. A few minutes with ice water in the glass makes a real difference.

**The lemon juice is not optional.** A small splash of fresh lemon juice cuts the sweetness and keeps the drink balanced. It's easy to skip; don't.

**On shaking vs. stirring:** bartenders will tell you shaking "bruises" the spirit. This is largely myth — alcohol doesn't bruise. What shaking does differently from stirring is introduce tiny air bubbles, which gives the drink a slightly frothy, diluted texture. For an appletini that's a fine trade. Shake it.

**Vodka quality matters more here than in most mixed drinks**, because vodka is most of the drink. Chopin is my preference. If you're on a budget and live near a Total Wine, their house brand (Starr Blu) is genuinely good for the price.

## Ingredients

- 2 oz vodka (Chopin preferred)
- 1 oz DeKuyper Apple Pucker
- Small splash of fresh lemon juice
- 1 maraschino cherry, for garnish (Luxardo if you can find them)

## Method

1. **Chill the glass** — Fill a martini glass with ice and cold water and set aside.

2. **Shake** — Combine vodka, Apple Pucker, and lemon juice in a shaker with ice. Shake 5–10 seconds until well chilled.

3. **Serve** — Discard the ice water from the glass. Strain the cocktail in and garnish with a cherry.

## The Elevated Version

Swap the Apple Pucker for Berentzen Apfelkorn at a **7:5 vodka-to-Berentzen ratio** (about 1¾ oz vodka to 1¼ oz Berentzen). Berentzen is a German apple schnapps — drier, less sweet, and more natural-tasting than Pucker. The result is a more grown-up drink: still recognizably an appletini, but with real apple character instead of candy sweetness. Everything else stays the same.
