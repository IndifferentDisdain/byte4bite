The best steak you'll ever cook at home isn't complicated — it's disciplined. Dry brine. Screaming hot cast iron. Real butter. Rest. That's it.

## Ingredients

- 2 bone-in ribeyes, 1¼–1½ inch thick
- Kosher salt
- Cracked black pepper
- 2 tbsp neutral oil (avocado or grapeseed)
- 3 tbsp unsalted butter
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- 2 sprigs rosemary

## Method

1. **Dry brine** — Season generously with kosher salt on all sides 1–24 hours ahead. The surface should be completely dry before it hits the pan.

2. **Bring to temp** — Pull steaks from the fridge 30 minutes before cooking. Season with cracked pepper right before they go in.

3. **Heat the pan** — Cast iron over high heat until it's screaming. Add oil and wait until it just starts to smoke.

4. **Sear** — Place steaks in the pan and don't touch them for 2 minutes. Flip once. Sear another 2 minutes. Sear the edges too — hold upright with tongs.

5. **Baste** — Reduce to medium. Add butter, smashed garlic, thyme, and rosemary. Tilt the pan and spoon the foaming butter continuously over the steaks for 1–2 minutes. This is not decorative.

6. **Oven finish** — For 1½-inch cuts, transfer (oven-safe pan) to a 400°F oven until the internal reads 125–130°F for medium-rare.

7. **Rest** — 5 minutes minimum, tented loosely with foil.

8. **Slice** — Off the bone, sliced against the grain.

## Notes

The basting step is where home cooks lose the thread. Spooning hot aromatic butter over the top cooks the steak from above while the cast iron works from below, and the garlic and herbs infuse the fat directly into the crust. Don't skip it.
