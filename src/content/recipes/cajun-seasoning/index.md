This is my everyday Cajun seasoning, and it lives in a spice shaker on my counter at all times. It goes on salmon, steak, shrimp, into chili, dirty rice, tacos, étouffée — anywhere you want depth and a little heat.

Most store-bought Cajun blends lead with salt. This one doesn't have any. That's intentional: when you control the salt separately, you can use as much seasoning as you want without over-salting the dish. It also means this blend works as a rub, a sauce seasoning, or a finishing sprinkle without throwing off your balance.

Each spice here has a job. Paprika (smoked if you can find it) provides the base — color, sweetness, and a gentle earthiness. Garlic powder and onion powder add savory depth. Black pepper brings bite. Cayenne brings heat, which you can dial up or down. Oregano and thyme round it out with the herbal backbone that gives Cajun food its complexity.

The fastest way to assemble this: lay a large sheet of parchment paper on the counter, measure everything onto it, fold the paper in half, and pour directly into your spice shaker. Set the shaker in a bowl to catch spills. Takes about 90 seconds.

Stored in a cool, dry place, this will hold its flavor for at least six months.

## Ingredients

- 2 tbsp paprika (smoked preferred)
- 2 tbsp garlic powder
- 2 tbsp black pepper, freshly ground
- 1 tbsp onion powder
- 1 tbsp cayenne pepper
- 1 tbsp dried oregano
- 1 tbsp dried thyme

## Method

1. **Mix** — Combine all ingredients. Pour into a spice shaker or airtight jar and store in a cool, dry place.

## Riffs

- Dial the cayenne up or down to control heat. A teaspoon instead of a tablespoon makes this very approachable; two tablespoons makes it genuinely hot.
- Add crushed red pepper for more heat with a slightly different character — it's a different kind of burn than cayenne.
- Add 2 tbsp of brown sugar for a sweeter blend that works especially well as a barbecue rub.
- For a rub, mix 1–2 tbsp of this with kosher salt and cracked pepper. The ratio depends on the protein and how aggressively you're seasoning.
